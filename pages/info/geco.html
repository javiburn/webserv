Gecko Appreciation Post

About Geckos

Geckos are a type of lizard found in warm climates throughout the world. They range from 1.6 cm to 60 cm. Geckos are unique among lizards in their vocalizations, making chirping sounds in social interactions with other geckos.

Gecko Lifestyle

Geckos are mostly small, usually nocturnal reptiles with a soft skin. They also possess a short stout body, a large head, and typically well-developed limbs. Many species are well known for their specialized toe pads that enable them to climb smooth and vertical surfaces, and even cross indoor ceilings with ease.

Gecko History

Geckos are believed to be the first lizards to vocalize, and some species can emit a variety of high-pitched sounds, including barks, chirps and clicks. Fossil evidence from the Middle East suggests geckos have been around for at least 100 million years, and they were likely the first lizards to make vocal sounds.
